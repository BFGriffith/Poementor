A sonnet is fundamentally a dialectical construct which allows the poet to examine the nature and ramifications of two usually contrasting ideas, emotions, states of mind, beliefs, actions, events, images, etc. — by juxtaposing the two against each other, and possibly resolving or just revealing the tensions created (and operating) between the two.
