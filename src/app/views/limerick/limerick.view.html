A limerick is a form of poetry traditionally written in one or more five-line stanzas, and using a predominantly anapestic meter (∪∪′) with a strict rhyme scheme (AABBA). Limmericks are also traditionally bawdy, ribald, or cleverly obscene — almost invariably written with humorous intent... The third and fourth lines of a limmerick are usually shorter than the other three. The form first appeared in England in the early years of the 18th century, and was popularized by Edward Lear in the 19th century.
